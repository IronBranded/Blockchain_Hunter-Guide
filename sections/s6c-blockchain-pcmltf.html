Blockchain PCMLTF

Proceeds of Crime and Terrorist Financing — the methodologies criminals use to move, anonymize, and exit illicit funds through blockchain infrastructure. The investigator's primary domain.

📌 PCMLTF Context
PCMLTF (Proceeds of Crime, Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing) is the Canadian legislative framework (PCMLTFA). Internationally equivalent terms: AML/CFT (Anti-Money Laundering / Countering the Financing of Terrorism). This section focuses specifically on blockchain-based methodologies across all three stages and their investigative countermeasures.

Methods
Red Flags
Investigation
Bitcoin ATMs
Cash-to-crypto kiosks. Early ATMs had no KYC. Regulatory pressure has pushed most to collect ID for transactions above thresholds — but structuring (multiple sub-threshold transactions) remains common. ATM operator records are subpoenable.
P2P Exchange Platforms
LocalBitcoins (shut down 2023), Paxful, Bisq — peer-to-peer markets where cash, gift cards, or bank transfers are exchanged for crypto. Limited KYC historically; now increasingly regulated but cash transactions still common.
Structuring (Smurfing)
Breaking large amounts into multiple sub-reporting-threshold transactions across multiple ATMs, VASPs, or P2P transactions to avoid triggering mandatory reporting (FINTRAC: $10K CAD; FinCEN: $10K USD). A criminal offense in itself.
Casino / Gaming Cycles
Illicit cash deposited as casino chips or online gaming credits, converted through minimal play, then withdrawn — increasingly in crypto. Some online casinos accept crypto deposits/withdrawals with minimal verification.
Complicit Business Integration
Illicit cash commingled with a cash-heavy business's revenue (food service, car wash, nail salon) then deposited legitimately and converted to crypto. The crypto purchase appears legitimate.
NFT Purchase (Self-Sale)
Creating an NFT and buying it from a self-controlled wallet — artificially establishing a "legitimate" art market transaction. The purchase price enters the ecosystem as nominally clean NFT sale proceeds.
ATM/P2P Red Flags
  • Multiple transactions just below reporting threshold at same ATM
  • Same recipient wallet across multiple ATM locations same day
  • Cash purchases with no plausible source of funds
  • Gift card payments for crypto (common in romance scam feeders)
VASP Account Red Flags
  • Account opened with immediate large deposit
  • Rapid purchase and withdrawal with no trading history
  • IP address inconsistent with stated location
  • Device fingerprint shared with other flagged accounts
1
ATM Operator Subpoena
Bitcoin ATM operators are VASPs subject to AML regulations. Subpoena for: customer ID submitted, transaction history for the wallet address, IP address of mobile app used, camera footage from ATM location.
2
Structuring Pattern Analysis
Map all transactions to the target wallet in the suspected structuring window. Aggregate amounts across multiple ATMs and VASPs. Documenting the pattern is essential for structuring charges independent of the underlying crime.
3
SAR / FINTRAC Correlation
Request whether any SARs have been filed for the target wallet or the entities transacting with it. FINTRAC/FinCEN SAR data may reveal patterns invisible in on-chain data alone.

Layering — Obscuring the Audit Trail
Chain-hopping · Mixers · DEX · Peeling chains · Anonymizing
Stage 2Investigator Focus
›
Methods
Anonymizing Tools
Investigation
Chain-Hopping
Moving assets across blockchains through bridges — BTC→ETH→TRON→XMR — forcing investigators to track across multiple tools and chain-specific explorers. Each bridge hop creates a forensic break that must be bridged using tools like Pulsy and Socketscan.
Mixer / Tumbler Use
  • Tornado Cash (ETH) — OFAC-sanctioned; Lazarus Group's preferred tool
  • Wasabi Wallet (BTC) — CoinJoin-based
  • ChipMixer (BTC) — seized by Europol March 2023
  • Sinbad.io (BTC) — OFAC-sanctioned Nov 2023
DEX Swaps
Swapping tokens on decentralized exchanges (Uniswap, Curve, Jupiter) with no KYC. The DEX itself cannot be subpoenaed — but all activity is on-chain. Investigators trace the swap transaction and analyze wallet behavior before/after the swap.
Peeling Chains
Sequential transaction cascade where each address forwards to the next with slightly less value — creating dozens of hops to exhaust investigative resources. Commercial tools (TRM Labs) trace these automatically; manual analysis requires systematic address-by-address documentation.
Instant / No-KYC Exchanges
ChangeNow, FixedFloat, SimpleSwap — swap services with no identity verification. No operator to subpoena. However, these services typically have consistent wallet infrastructure that can be identified and tracked across multiple investigations.
NFT Wash Trading
Buying NFTs between self-controlled wallets at inflated prices to create transaction records establishing "legitimate" provenance for funds. The NFT then appears to have earned its purchase price through the art market.
VPN / TOR
Obscures real IP address during VASP logins and blockchain interactions. Spur.us identifies VPN/TOR IPs in VASP subpoena returns. Residential proxy services are harder to detect but leave billing trails.
Non-Custodial Wallets
MetaMask, Phantom, Ledger — wallets where the user holds private keys. No central custodian to subpoena. However, the wallet address itself is on-chain and investigable. The challenge is linking the address to a real identity.
Privacy Coins at Bridges
Converting to Monero (XMR) at a bridge point creates the hardest forensic break. The Monero transaction is opaque — but the bridge-in and bridge-out transactions remain on the public blockchains on either side, creating investigable chokepoints.
Multi-Sig Wallets
Requiring multiple private keys to authorize a transaction — distributing control across multiple jurisdictions or individuals to complicate seizure. The Bybit hack exploited a compromised multi-sig infrastructure rather than bypassing it.
1
Systematic Hop Documentation
Document every address in the layering chain with timestamps, amounts, and counterparties. Each hop is an evidence exhibit. Use TRM Labs graphs for automatic multi-hop visualization. Screenshot and archive at each step.
2
Bridge Tracking Tools
Use Pulsy, Socketscan, and chain-specific bridge explorers to follow cross-chain transfers. Identify source and destination of every bridge transaction — the bridge-out address is investigable on the destination chain.
3
Mixer Analysis
When funds enter a mixer, use output timing analysis and amount correlation (funds going in vs. amounts coming out in similar denominations) to identify likely output addresses. Tornado Cash withdrawal patterns have been analyzed to deanonymize outputs in multiple cases.
4
Privacy Coin Chokepoints
Even if XMR internal transactions are opaque, trace what entered and exited the XMR ecosystem. Exchange deposits of XMR require KYC at compliant VASPs. Find the exchange where XMR was purchased or cashed out and serve legal process.

Integration & Exit — Cashing Out "Clean" Funds
OTC brokers · Fiat ramps · Real estate · Luxury assets
Stage 3Exit Point
›
Exit Methods
OTC Brokers
Investigation
OTC Broker Fiat Conversion
Over-the-counter brokers facilitate large crypto-to-fiat conversions outside of formal exchange infrastructure. Some operate with minimal AML controls. The Khinkis case (March 2026) documented $4.7M+ in ransomware proceeds routed through a single OTC broker's exchange account.
Centralized Exchange Cash-Out
Depositing layered crypto to a regulated VASP and withdrawing fiat to a bank account. The KYC at this stage is the critical investigative chokepoint — straw identities and mule accounts are commonly used to obscure the beneficial owner.
Real Estate
Purchasing property with crypto-origin funds — increasingly regulated in many jurisdictions but enforcement gaps remain. Real estate provides high-value integration and an appreciating asset. FINTRAC requires real estate professionals to report large cash/crypto transactions.
Luxury Asset Purchases
Watches, vehicles, jewelry, artwork, and yachts purchased with crypto are easily converted to cash or held as stores of value. ZachXBT identified $243M BTC theft suspects through their social media display of luxury purchases inconsistent with legitimate income.
Business Investment
Illicit crypto invested into legitimate businesses — restaurants, tech startups, retail — to create an ongoing legitimate revenue stream. The business's income appears legitimate; the criminal's wealth is now "clean" and generating returns.
Crypto Payroll
Paying criminal network members (affiliates, mules, developers) directly in crypto as "salary" — avoiding bank compliance and distributing funds with transaction records that mimic legitimate employment payments.

OTC brokers are frequently the final cashout point for major crypto criminal networks. They operate in legal grey zones — some are licensed MSBs, others are entirely unlicensed. Their business model: facilitating large crypto-fiat conversions for clients who cannot or will not use regulated exchanges.

Identifying OTC Brokers
  • On-chain: large consolidation wallets receiving from multiple criminal sources
  • TRM Labs "Know Your VASP" database includes known OTC operators
  • Forum intelligence: darknet marketplaces advertise OTC services
  • Advertising on Telegram channels alongside other criminal services
Khinkis Case Study
ZachXBT's March 2026 investigation identified Russian OTC broker "Aleks" Khinkis routing $4.7M+ in ransomware proceeds since July 2025. Traced through BTC→AVA bridge analysis and Tron network correlation. Confirmed via OSINT on Khinkis's travel patterns.
Legal Process on OTC
  • If registered VASP/MSB: subpoena for KYC records, customer list, transaction history
  • If on regulated exchange: subpoena the exchange for OTC account records
  • If unregistered: criminal referral for unlicensed money services
Jurisdictional Challenges
Many OTC brokers operate from low-regulation jurisdictions (Southeast Asia, Eastern Europe, Middle East). MLAT requests or Interpol coordination may be required. Tether freeze requests can freeze USDT balances across jurisdictions instantly.
1
Identify the Exit VASP
Trace the final crypto transaction before fiat conversion. The receiving VASP (exchange, OTC) is where the criminal will have KYC records. Issue emergency freeze and subpoena simultaneously — funds may be withdrawn during compliance window.
2
Follow Fiat Proceeds
Once fiat is identified: request bank records for the account receiving crypto sale proceeds. Suspicious cash deposits or large transfers into luxury goods / real estate become additional evidence of money laundering.
3
Asset Tracing for Forfeiture
Map all assets purchased with proceeds — real estate titles, vehicle registrations, bank accounts. Each asset purchased with traceable illicit crypto is subject to forfeiture. Work with prosecutors to file civil forfeiture actions concurrently with criminal charges.

Terrorist Financing via Blockchain
Crowdfunding · Sanctions evasion · State-sponsored
National SecurityOFAC Sanctions
›
Overview
Methods
Investigation

Terrorist organizations use cryptocurrency to raise, move, and spend funds — exploiting crypto's borderless, censorship-resistant properties to evade traditional financial surveillance. Methods range from direct public wallet solicitation (Hamas, ISIS) to complex sanctions-evasion infrastructure (North Korea's Lazarus Group generating state revenue through crypto theft).

OFAC (U.S. Treasury) maintains a list of sanctioned wallet addresses. Transactions with OFAC-listed addresses — even indirectly — may create legal liability for VASPs and individuals. The Tornado Cash sanctions (2022) established that smart contract addresses themselves can be sanctioned entities.

Public Solicitation
Publishing donation wallet addresses on social media, dark web sites, or encrypted messaging platforms. The Al-Sadaqah case (US DOJ) involved a terror financing entity publishing a Bitcoin donation address on social media — immediately traceable to a VASP deposit.
State-Sponsored Theft
North Korea's Lazarus Group steals cryptocurrency to fund the DPRK nuclear program — bypassing sanctions through direct theft rather than requiring donor networks. $2B+ stolen in 2025 alone. Assessed by US intelligence as generating significant state revenue.
Sanctions Evasion
Using crypto to conduct commerce in sanctioned jurisdictions (Iran, Russia, DPRK) by bypassing SWIFT and traditional banking. OTC brokers and P2P networks facilitate sanctions-evading transactions.
Hawala-Crypto Hybrids
Combining traditional hawala informal value transfer networks with crypto to move funds internationally while maintaining plausible deniability at each node. Funds enter as crypto, exit as fiat in the destination jurisdiction through a hawala broker.
1
OFAC Sanctions Check
Run all addresses through OFAC's SDN list and commercial tools' sanctions screening. Any address with direct or indirect exposure to a sanctioned entity creates immediate legal process justification and potential VASP reporting obligations.
2
Direct VASP Exposure Mapping
As in the Al-Sadaqah case: input the solicitation address, identify direct counterparty exposure to a compliant VASP, and issue legal process for KYC. The fastest path to attribution is often the shortest path to a subpoenable custodian.
3
National Security Escalation
Terrorist financing investigations require immediate escalation to national security agencies (CSIS, FBI JTTF, NSA). Evidence handling requirements differ from financial crime investigations. Coordinate with legal counsel on classification requirements before documenting.
🚨 Critical
Terrorist financing investigations require immediate law enforcement notification and coordination with national security agencies. Do not conduct independent action that could compromise intelligence collection or ongoing operations.
