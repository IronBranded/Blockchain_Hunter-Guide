The $243M Bitcoin Theft — ZachXBT's First Paid Case
$243M
📅 DATE: August 2024
🎯 METHOD: Social Engineering — Coinbase & Google Support Impersonation
✅ OUTCOME: DOJ arrested Malone Lam & Jeandiel Serrano, Sept 18, 2024
Over 4,000 BTC stolen from one victim — a creditor of Genesis Trading — after fraudsters posed as Coinbase and Google support to obtain private keys. Perpetrators (Malone Lam, aka "Anne Hathaway," and Jeandiel Serrano, aka "VersaceGod") impersonated Coinbase support agents, then directed the victim to a spoofed Google call to create the appearance of a coordinated security alert. ZachXBT identified suspects partly through social media behavior — posting luxury purchases, nightclub appearances, and extravagant spending entirely inconsistent with any legitimate income source. A fourth suspect (Danish Zulfiqar) was arrested in Dubai in December 2025.
Key Investigation Techniques
On-chain tracing: followed 4,000+ BTC from victim wallet through complex laundering chain
Social media OSINT: suspects publicly posted luxury spending — cars, clubs, jewelry — inconsistent with known income
Timeline correlation: social media timestamps matched timing of illicit fund movements
$9M+ frozen by zeroShadow, Cryptoforensic Investigators, and Binance Security
Lesson: Timing Analysis + Location OSINT
Haby — Coinbase Impersonation Scammer
$2M+
📅 DATE: Late 2024 — Published Early 2025
🎯 LOCATION: Abbotsford, British Columbia, Canada
On December 30, 2024, a scammer ("Haby") posted a screenshot on social media showing a 21,000 XRP theft from a Coinbase user. ZachXBT matched the wallet address to two additional thefts totaling $500K+. Stolen XRP was swapped to Bitcoin through instant exchanges. Timing analysis tracked the Bitcoin address, and OSINT confirmed the suspect's physical location through Instagram story metadata. Suspect deleted their Telegram account 2 days before the investigation was published — but archived screenshots preserved all evidence. Note: this case predates the May 2025 Coinbase insider data breach; Haby operated through direct social engineering rather than breach-derived customer data.
OSINT Techniques That Cracked the Case
Instagram story posts contained location data confirming Abbotsford, BC — poor operational security
Timing analysis: exact timestamps of XRP-to-BTC swaps correlated with suspect's online activity
Historic Telegram chat screenshots preserved previous aliases before account deletion
Archive.today snapshots preserved evidence before suspect's deletion campaign
Lesson: Behavioral OSINT + Private Channel Intelligence
ZachXBT Exposes USMS Asset Theft
$46M
📅 DATE: Late January 2026
🎯 TARGET: US Marshals Service (USMS) Government Custody Wallets
ZachXBT obtained a recording of a dispute in a private Telegram group where two individuals attempted to prove who controlled more cryptocurrency. One participant screen-shared a wallet holding ~$2.3M and then transferred $6.7M in ETH in real time — inadvertently demonstrating control over addresses traced back to USMS government custody wallets. Subject identified as John Daghita, son of Dean Daghita, president of CMDSS (a USMS contract holder).
Investigation Highlights
Key technique: real-time screen-share in Telegram provided live view of government wallet activity — unintentional self-incrimination
Wallet addresses in screen-share traced back to USMS government custody infrastructure
Corporate and family connections identified via OSINT on government contract records
Primary lesson: private group chats and social media bragging remain the most consistent OSINT source across all investigations
Lesson: Bridge Analysis + Funding Source Tracing
Hyperliquid Whale — Cybercriminal Gambling With Stolen Funds
$20M
📅 DATE: Early 2025
🎯 PLATFORM: Hyperliquid (Decentralized Perps Exchange)
An anonymous trader made $20M+ on Hyperliquid using high-leverage ETH and BTC positions with suspiciously well-timed entries. ZachXBT identified this as a cybercriminal gambling with stolen funds, tracing capital back to a January 2025 casino exploit and phishing operation. The whale's deliberate $200M+ ETH liquidation caused $4M to drain from Hyperliquid's liquidity vault.
Key Investigative Insights
On-chain profitability analysis revealed DeFi positions funded from known illicit sources
Cross-chain tracing connected DeFi trading profits back to prior phishing victims
OSINT Industries used to correlate wallet activity with suspect identity data
Lesson: large, unusual DeFi positions always warrant investigation of funding source origin
